## Defining "Enterprise"

To start, we need the foundation of a consistent understanding of "Enterprise" and I suggest we view it from this perspective:

> Software or Hardware tools **provided by employers** that **enable** employees to **do their jobs**.

Following the emphasized lines above, Enterprise technology is:
* managed
* integrated
* powerful

## The Misinformation Era
Starting with integrated, Apple device admins have spent years hearing the bite of sarcasm when our counterparts from other platforms retort with "it just works" in reference to our devices. To be clear, Apple devices are not void of integration challenges and at the same time, I consistently see those challenges met with positivity from fellow Mac Admins.

> Macs don't work in our environment

Well, what are we usually talking about here? Identity, infrastructure, or maybe even software. With or without an MDM, Macs can bind to Active Directory or use some incredible open source services like NoMAD. Not only do both of these options cover identity services integration, but also domain services like file sharing and printing. Recently, at the 2016 WWDC keynote, Apple and Cisco announced their partnership which has eliminated any questions about wireless infrastructure compatibility. Lastly, virtualization and native Intel support means that your users can run the archaic, single-platform software you require through a Boot Camp native Windows installation or virtualized along side of their Mac apps.
